Executive Summary

The Picture at a Glance

Our work answers four questions about the future of upsell. Great means a signal-driven, AI-native motion that surfaces, qualifies, and acts on expansion opportunities automatically — turning installed-base whitespace and regulatory change into predictable revenue. Getting there hinges on data and delivery readiness, a set of new capabilities, and resolving a handful of open questions about automation, ownership, and governance.

4 · Future-State Statements

In 2–3 years, what world-class looks like for this motion.

The model will continuously monitor regulatory, product, and account signals to automatically surface upsell opportunities — no manual hunting.
The model will auto-qualify each opportunity against health, usage, coverage, fit, and delivery-readiness prerequisites before a seller ever engages.
The model will rank and prioritize opportunities across the book when multiple accounts qualify, so effort goes to the highest-value plays first.
The model will auto-generate the sales play — targeted messaging, indicative pricing, presentation, and script — tailored to persona and account context.
Clients will experience timely, relevant outreach that anticipates needs created by regulatory and ecosystem change (e.g., Basel) rather than generic product pitches.
Sellers will experience a guided, repeatable play from trigger to signing, with follow-up and nurturing driven by captured meeting insights.
Nasdaq will convert existing whitespace into expansion revenue faster and more predictably, with full traceability from signal to closed deal.
Reality Check

5 · Key Constraints & Opportunities

What stands in the way — and what's working in our favor — on the path to this future state.

▲ Opportunities

  • Reg-driven demandRegulatory change (e.g., Basel) is a high-confidence, repeatable trigger mapped to our Reg Reporting footprint — a built-in reason to buy now.
  • Installed-base whitespaceExpansion into existing accounts is lower-CAC than net-new; whitespace and new features create ready-made upsell paths.
  • AI-native leverageMessaging, presentations, indicative pricing, and scripts can be auto-generated, collapsing time from signal to outreach.
  • Cross-account prioritizationRanking qualified accounts focuses effort on the highest-value plays first.

▼ Constraints

  • Signal / data availabilityTriggers depend on clean, integrated data across reg feeds, product usage, CRM, contracts, and entitlements.
  • Delivery capacityA booked upsell still needs services capacity to fulfill — selling ahead of delivery erodes the win.
  • Product applicability mappingNeed a structured catalog that maps which modules fit which account profile.
  • Relationship integrityOver-automated outreach risks eroding the trusted-advisor relationship.

6 · Capability Needs

What we need in place to operate this model.

Signal / trigger engine

Monitors regulatory feeds, product usage, new-feature releases, and account signals; fires qualified triggers.

Unified data layer

CRM + contracts + entitlements + usage in one place, so prerequisites can be auto-checked.

Opportunity scoring & ranking

The "value check" as a model: thresholds that qualify an account and rank across the book.

Product catalog & applicability

Structured mapping of which module fits which account profile.

Automated play generation

Messaging, presentation, script, and indicative pricing tailored to persona and account.

Pricing service

Indicative pricing surfaced safely, without undercutting negotiation.

Capture → follow-up

Meeting recording feeds the next action automatically.

Account intelligence

Stakeholder maps, engagement history, and account insights.

Governance / human-in-the-loop

Compliance review for AI-generated, reg-related outreach.

To Resolve

7 · Open Questions & Tensions

What we still need to work through.

Automation vs. relationship — how much of the motion can be AI-driven before it erodes the trusted-advisor relationship?
Buy vs. build — do we build the signal engine and data layer, or buy?
Data readiness — is our contract / entitlement / usage data clean enough to auto-qualify accounts today?
Motion ownership — who runs this: CS, Sales, or a dedicated expansion team?
Compliance exposure — reg-themed outreach (e.g., Basel) needs a review path; who signs off?
Pricing transparency — how much indicative pricing do we expose before it weakens our negotiating position?
Qualification thresholds — what concretely defines a "qualified" opportunity at the value-check gate?
Prioritization — when capacity is limited, do we favor new-module cross-sell or deepening existing footprint?
